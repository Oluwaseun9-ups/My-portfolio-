        <h2>A Chance Encounter in a Crowded Place</h2>
        <p>The clock on my phone showed 7:43 AM. Seventeen minutes until my interview, and I was still fighting through the chaos of Oshodi bus terminal. My carefully pressed shirt was already starting to stick to my back in the morning heat. Everyone seemed to be moving in slow motion except me – market women with baskets balanced on their heads, school children in perfectly ironed uniforms, and office workers like myself, all of us doing this morning dance of dodge and weave. That's when it happened. In my rush to check the time again, my shoulder slammed hard into someone, sending both our phones clattering to the ground.</p>
        
        <p>"I'm so sorry about that," I said, hurriedly bending down to retrieve both our phones. That's when I noticed the other person's phone had a cracked screen. "I was in a rush and wasn't watching where I was going."</p>
        
        <p>The other person, a smartly dressed woman in her 30s, gave a weary sigh as she examined the phone. "Don't worry about it. These things happen."</p>
        
        <p>She looked up, locking eyes with me. There was a brief pause as we took each other in. I couldn't help but notice how tired yet determined her expression was.</p>
        
        <p>"Are you alright?" she asked. "You seem a bit frazzled."</p>
        
        <p>I glanced at the bus that had just pulled away. "Actually, no. I'm supposed to be at a job interview right now, and I just missed my bus."</p>
        
        <p>The woman's brow furrowed with empathy. "Oh dear, that's not good. Is there anything I can do to help?"</p>
        
        <p>That's when it hit me. "Wait a minute, I know you! You're Amara Nwosu, the editor-in-chief at Vanity Fair. I just applied for that writing position you had open!"</p>
        
        <p>Amara's eyes widened in recognition. "Of course, I remember your application! You have an impressive portfolio." She paused, a small smile playing on her lips. "And now I'm the one who's made you miss your interview with us. This is quite the unexpected encounter."</p>
        
        <p>I felt my heart start to race. This was the opportunity of a lifetime - to meet the woman who could make or break my career, here of all places. "I can't believe this. I was so nervous about that interview, and now..."</p>
        
        <p>Amara held up a hand. "Don't worry, we can fix this. Come with me, I'll make sure you get there on time."</p>
        
        <p>I stared at her, dumbfounded. "You'd do that? For me?"</p>
        
        <p>"Consider it making up for my clumsiness earlier." She winked, already turning to lead the way. "Now hurry, we don't want you to be late."</p>
